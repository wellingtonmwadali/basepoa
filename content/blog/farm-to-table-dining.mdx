# Farm-to-Table in Tigoni

For decades, Tigoni was known for one thing: Tea. But in recent years, a quiet culinary revolution has taken place. 

Because of the rich volcanic soil and the cool, damp climate, the farms around Limuru and Tigoni produce some of the highest-quality organic vegetables and dairy in East Africa. And now, chefs are moving to the highlands to take advantage of it.

## The Dairy Capital
You cannot talk about food in Tigoni without mentioning **Brown’s Cheese**. What started as a small family operation has turned into an internationally recognized artisan dairy. Their tasting tours have proven that Kenya can produce camembert and gouda that rivals Europe.

## Weekend Pop-Ups
The real magic of the Tigoni food scene lies in the weekend pop-ups. Places like the **Tigoni Farm Kitchen** operate exclusively on weekends, serving set menus dictated entirely by what was harvested that morning. 
Imagine eating a slow-roasted highland lamb with heirloom carrots, sitting at a rustic wooden table right next to the garden where the carrots grew. 

## The Classics 
Of course, the historic institutions still hold their own. The **Kentmere Club** continues to serve classic, hearty colonial-style roasts, while **Muna Tree Café** has mastered the art of the perfect, wood-fired sourdough pizza using local mozzarella. 

If you are a foodie, Tigoni should be at the very top of your weekend getaway list.
