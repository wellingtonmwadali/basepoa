# A Brief History of Tigoni

When you drive up into the Tigoni highlands and see the perfectly manicured tea fields rolling over the hills, it feels as though the landscape has been this way forever. But the history of Tigoni is a fascinating story of agricultural experimentation, colonial history, and modern resurgence.

## The Early Days

In the early 1900s, British settlers discovered that the high altitude (over 2,200m), rich volcanic soils, and reliable rainfall of the Limuru and Tigoni area created the perfect conditions for growing tea. Before this, the area was heavily forested and used largely by the local Kikuyu communities. 

The first tea seeds were planted as an experiment, and they thrived. Soon, sprawling estates began to carve out the landscape.

## The Growth of the Estates

By the mid-20th century, Tigoni was home to some of the largest tea estates in East Africa. Grand colonial homes (some of which now serve as boutique hotels like the **Kentmere Club**) were built on the ridges to oversee the valleys. The area became a hub for expatriates and agricultural pioneers.

## Modern Tigoni: The Boutique Escape

Today, while the massive commercial tea estates still exist, Tigoni has undergone a renaissance. It has transformed from a purely agricultural hub into Kenya's premier highland retreat destination. 

Farm owners have opened their doors to the public, offering guided walks and tastings. Old farmhouses have been converted into luxury stays like **Thayu House** and **Siri Mitini**. And a new wave of culinary entrepreneurs has turned the area into a farm-to-table dining destination.

When you visit Tigoni today, you are stepping into a living piece of Kenyan history—one that is evolving beautifully with the times.
