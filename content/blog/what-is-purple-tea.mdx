# What is Purple Tea?

If you book a Farm Tour in Tigoni, you will inevitably be offered a cup of something highly unusual: **Purple Tea**. 

Kenya is the largest exporter of black tea in the world, but in recent years, the highlands of Tigoni have become ground zero for a completely new, naturally occurring varietal. 

## The Origins
Purple tea isn't dyed or artificially flavored. The leaves of this specific tea bush naturally grow with a deep purplish-red hue. This coloring comes from **anthocyanins**—the exact same super-antioxidants that give blueberries and eggplants their color. 

The Tea Research Foundation of Kenya spent 25 years developing this unique varietal to thrive in the high-altitude, high-UV environment of places like Tigoni.

## The Health Benefits
Because it is packed with anthocyanins, Purple Tea boasts incredibly high antioxidant levels—even higher than traditional green tea. It is praised for its anti-inflammatory properties and its ability to boost the immune system.

## The Tasting Notes
So, what does it actually taste like? 

Unlike black tea, which can be bitter and requires milk, or green tea, which can be grassy, purple tea is incredibly smooth. 
*   **The Profile:** It has a light, earthy flavor with subtle sweet notes of plum and wild berries. 
*   **The Magic Trick:** If you squeeze a few drops of lemon juice into your cup, the acidity reacts with the anthocyanins, and the tea will instantly change color from a dark purple to a bright, vibrant pink!

Next time you are at the **Tea Estate Terrace** or on a farm tour, make sure to ask for a pot of purple tea.
